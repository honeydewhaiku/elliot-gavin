scatter/brain

Sometimes the riddle of her kiss still tingles on your tongue, metallic, like the round white tablets when they catch on the roof of your mouth, leaking lithium salt into your gums. Raindrops drum against the window, leaving wet trails on the cool surface of the glass.

Once you prayed catch the stars in my eyes, like rain in a bucket, like fireflies in a jar. You looked up at the infinitely vast expanse of space and started to walk a slow revolution about her, proclaiming that she would be your sun and you would be her planet.

It’s winter now and the fireflies are dormant, dead to the dark and cold. You pray to no-one. On your knees, you bleed black ink onto the page: a portrait of the kiss you mistook for a kiss.
