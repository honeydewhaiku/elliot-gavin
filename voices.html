voices

by Julie Scarr


1.
whispers in the night:
you will love
and you will lose all,
they tell me.
I want to become
more than a disease,
somewhere inside, I inwardly voice.
not now, says he or she.
god, nobody knows.
time and time again
it is not time.
never.
and god, the sky
is full of a sudden
crash in the night
which engulfs and shouts
'illness;
you shall not overcome.’
I hear the voices. Yet, I hear
patterns in the music;
"little hope," iamamiwhoami,
her voice,
sings to me softly
and I wonder if it is
all that I will ever have.
I pray.
2.
One foot
Steps
In front
And
Then
Back.
Auditory hallucination; held at a confident and consistent pitch. But inside, this voice.
It is inside; in depth, some say embedded in imagination.
But oh, the shout is so ‘r-e-a-l,’ how it conveys that I will no longer be moving forward.
Yet I fight,
I run,
I argue against its very existence.

‘Fuck.
You.
Don’t you tell me.
Don’t you enter into my brain without due permission.
Don’t you pretend that I am not worth it.’

I think I am weak but all in a burst I recall that I was told to die by my own hand, by a force unknown embodied in voice.
